
=== CATEGORY: BOSSES ===
This page lists all V Blood Carriers. for a list of all non-V Blood Enemies, see its respective page.
Tracking V Bloods is done through the V Blood Menu (default key K) after completing the Journal quest Getting Ready for the Hunt. At 50% health, V Bloods spawn at least 3 Blood Orbs, restoring 7.5% max health on pick-up.
On Relaxed Difficulty they have 20% less health & deal 40% less damage. On Brutal Difficulty they have 25% more health, deal 70% more damage, are 3 levels higher and often have more abilities and combat behaviours.

Throwable Potions (Exclusive to Oakveil Woodlands)[]
Beauty Elixir - Polymorphs all targets in the area into Sheep. Is required to end Lucile's "Alchemic Madness" state.
Liquid Fire - Sets the area and all targets entering it on fire. The burn makes Gold Golems vulnerable to attacks for the duration. Instantly kills Corrupted Mantrap.
Pixies Luck - Dramatically increases player movement speed.
Potion of Dispel - Knocks targets back and dispels all effects on targets hit, including Lucile's "Gold Skin" invulnerability.
Rejuvenation Juice - Applies a healing buff to targets in the area. Taking damage ends the effect.

=== CATEGORY: BLOOD_TYPES ===
"You will slowly lose health and ultimately perish if you run out of blood."
Example of a Frail Type Blood Pool; no bonuses.
Blood is a key resource in V Rising.
The player must continuously find new victims lest they run out of blood entirely. Feeding on a blood-carrying target (victim) will refill the player's Blood Pool to its maximum capacity of 10 liters and absorb the victim's blood type (Creature, Rogue, Warrior, etc.). Later on in the game the player unlocks Blood Potion and Blood Merlot, which allows them to store blood for later consumption. Each blood type provides different passive bonuses, with higher quality blood granting stronger bonuses. The 5 tiers of Blood Pool powers are unlocked at different percentage levels. For example, Tier IV Blood Pool powers will only be unlocked by draining a 90%+ blood quality victim.
The Blood Pool drains slowly over time, and faster when dealing & taking damage or using particular vampire powers such as Blood Mend or Expose Vein. Dying will immediately incur a 1 liter Blood Pool loss. Blood drainage can be virtually paused whilst sleeping in a Coffin. Blood drain is reduced by 25% on Relaxed Difficulty.Upon running out of a non-frailed Blood Pool, the player's Blood Pool will revert to 1 liter of  Frailed blood. If their frailed Blood Pool runs out, the player's health will slowly deplete to 1 health. Feeding on any source of blood will restore the player's health regeneration.
Prior to v1.0, a player's current Blood Pool could not be refilled by any means. However, as of update 1.0, it is now possible by feeding on a defeated Primal Blood Soul, either from a Tier 2 Rift Incursion in the Ruins of Mortium or a summoned one from the Stygian Summoning Circle. Doing so will refill the player's current Blood Pool by 5 liters (50%).

Bonus Scaling[]
For tiers I - III, at least one variable is proportional to the quality of the given blood, down to a minimum of half the max potential bonus. For example,  Worker Blood's Tier 1 bonus will provide at least 12% but up-to 24% Increased Resource yield, the value of which ranges between 1% and 100% Blood Quality:
1% Blood Quality => 12% Increased Resource yield
30% Blood Quality => 15.6% Increased Resource yield
50% Blood Quality => 18% Increased Resource yield
99% Blood Quality => 24% Increased Resource yield
100% Blood Quality => ~29% Increased Resource yield (including Tier V bonus of +20%)
Detecting Blood Types[]
Example of the Blood Hunger ability with the blood type icon and percentage displayed.
Mouse over a victim to display the blood type and blood quality percentage. See Enemies for a list of all enemies with a blood type.
Once the player has fed on the V Blood Carrier Tristan the Vampire Hunter, they will unlock the Blood Hunger ability. Blood Hunger places the player in a blood detection mode, displaying the blood type icon and percentage over all victims' heads, instead of needing to mouse over.
Blood Consumables[]
The player can consume items such as Rats and Tainted Hearts to restore a small amount of their blood pool, at the cost of changing their blood type to 'frailed', losing all blood type bonuses.
Once the player has fed on the V Blood Carrier Vincent the Frostbringer, they will unlock Prison Cells. By using the Dominate Vampire Power they can imprison high-quality human victims and repeatedly extract Blood Potions / Blood Merlots from them. Allowing for a consistent supply of high quality blood.
Once the player has fed on Lucile the Venom Alchemist, they will additionally be able to mix Blood Potions via the Blood Homogenizer. This allows for fusing one of the blood traits in a potion into another. If the blood quality of the secondary potion is 90% or higher, the IV bonus will also be infused.
Servants increase in power and effectiveness with blood quality level. For more info see Servants.
